Jorge Eliecer Gaitan

The man who could have transformed Colombia

"I am not a man, I am the people, and the people are greater than their leaders"

Ten key themes in the ideology of Jorge Eliécer Gaitán

  • 1. The Economy

    " - We do not say that man should be a slave to the economy, we say that the economy should be at the service of man." "We must ensure that the rich are less rich and the poor, less poor. "

  • 2. the Peace

    Lord, prevent violence. We want the defense of human life, which is what a people can ask for. Instead of this blind force unleashed, we must take advantage of the people's capacity to work for the benefit of Colombia's progress ”. (He was addressing President Mariano Ospina, in the March of Silence).

  • 3. Education

    "An ignorant people are victims of misunderstanding and laziness."

  • 4. The People

    "The people are superior to their leaders." "unfortunate are the rulers who hide impiety for the men of their people behind the goodness of their words ...". "Because the Colombian government has murderous shrapnel for the people and is knee-deep in the face of American gold."

  • 5. Self-Vision

    "I am not a man, I am a people." "I don't think that no matter how great the individual qualities are, there is no one capable of making their passions, their thoughts or their determinations the passion, determination and thought of the collective soul."

  • 6. Prophecy

    “This human avalanche: fight a battle, you will fight a battle; it will defeat the liberal oligarchy and crush the conservative oligarchy ”.

  • 7. Prophecy that was partially fulfilled

    "No hand of the people will rise against me and the oligarchy will not kill me, because it knows that if it does, the country will turn over and the waters will take fifty years to return to normal."

  • 8. Justice

    "He who sentences a case without hearing the opposing party, even if he judges what is fair, that sentence is unfair."

  • 9. Corruption

    "People, for moral restoration, charge!"

  • 10. Commitment and death

    “If I advance, follow me. If I stop, push me. If I betray you, kill me. If I die, avenge me ”.
